Learning AI products by making prototypes 通过做原型学习 AI 产品

Notes on turning product ideas, AI workflows, and interface references into concrete product attempts. 记录如何把产品想法、AI 工作流和界面参考变成一次次具体尝试。

Musu May 14, 2026  ·  6 min read阅读 6 分钟

I am still learning what it means to think like an AI product manager. For now, the most useful path is simple: notice a workflow, make a small prototype, use it, and then ask what changed.

我还在学习什么才是真正的 AI 产品思考。对现在的我来说，最有用的方法很朴素：观察一个工作流，做一个小原型，自己试着用，再回头问它到底改变了什么。

Start from a real workflow

从真实工作流开始

A good prototype does not have to start from a big feature list. It can start from a small moment: opening a paper, saving a useful paragraph, trying to explain a concept, or turning a vague research idea into a structured draft.

一个好的原型不一定要从很大的功能清单开始。它可以从一个很小的时刻开始：打开一篇论文、保存一段有用文字、尝试解释一个概念，或者把一个模糊的研究想法整理成结构化草稿。

"A prototype is useful when it makes an idea easier to question, not when it pretends the idea is already finished." "原型真正有用的地方，不是证明一个想法已经完成，而是让这个想法更容易被追问。"

When I look at an AI product idea, I try to separate it into three questions:

当我看一个 AI 产品想法时，我会先把它拆成三个问题：

  • Workflow: What is the user already trying to do?工作流：用户本来就在尝试完成什么事情？
  • Interface: What does the AI need to make visible, editable, and trustworthy?界面：AI 生成的东西，哪些需要可见、可编辑、可信任？
  • Judgment: Where should the person stay in control?判断：哪些地方仍然必须由人来决定？

What I am practicing

我正在练习什么

I am using projects like PaperForge and WebLearnBoost to practice product thinking. The technical stack matters, but it is not the center. The more important exercise is learning how to make an uncertain idea concrete enough to test.

我正在借助 PaperForge 和 WebLearnBoost 这样的项目练习产品思考。技术栈当然重要，但它不是中心。更重要的是学习如何把一个不确定的想法做得足够具体，让它可以被试用和检验。

Right now, I care less about having perfect answers and more about building loops where ideas can become visible, usable, and easier to improve. 现在的我更在意的不是立刻拥有完美答案，而是建立一种循环：让想法变得可见、可用，并且更容易被改进。

This note is less like a finished article and more like a marker for where I am. I want this site to slowly collect those markers: prototypes, observations, small mistakes, and things I learned by actually making something.

这篇记录不像一篇完成度很高的文章，更像是我现在所在位置的一个标记。我希望这个网站慢慢收集这些标记：原型、观察、小错误，以及真正动手之后学到的东西。
